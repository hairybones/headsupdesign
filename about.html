Passion to Profession

There's no feeling quite like turning what you do for the love of doing it into something that you can do, not only for the love of it, but as a way to live as well. Passion is the name of the game at Heads Up Design. If you aren't doing what you're passionate about, people will notice. That's why we've only ever created work with a passion for it; so we can be able to create the best work we possibly can.

Harrison Eads: Behind the Name

While still young, around a decade of experience creating these kinds of works is no joke. I started what I'm doing today from a very young age doing simple videos for YouTube with Adobe Premiere Essentials and art using Paint.net. Everything after was just built upon the constant experience. I still do YouTube videos and random art pieces, but I have much more skill, a better sense of artistic balance, and professional software to help me accomplish my goals.

Where it all began

You can cite the beginning of any passion to any point in time. I can tell you I started drawing similar "graphical art" as long as I could remember. Graphic design didn't really become a larger part of my life until I started devoting much of my free time to YouTube at around age 12. I forced myself to make content to edit, learn to edit well, and design graphics for both the videos themselves and the YouTube thumbnails. While I never got "famous", nor is that really a goal of mine, it still is a part of who I am and a good measure of how far I've come as a professional in graphic design and other related fields. There's awlays room to improve, so I always like to make sure I have to the room to do so.
